  This paper develops a model in which the composition of public debt across domestic banks, domestic non-banks, and foreign investors is determined by endogenous borrowing limits. The government faces default constraints on non-bank creditors and convex costs of financial repression on bank-held debt. These constraints generate threshold effects: when total debt exceeds a critical level, the government exhausts non-bank borrowing capacity and allocates marginal debt to banks.

We structurally estimate the model using maximum likelihood on panel data of debt composition. The results identify distinct borrowing limits across creditor types and provide a tractable framework to interpret cross-country and time variation in who holds government debt.
