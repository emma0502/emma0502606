\section{Conclusion}\whenintegrated{\label{conclusion}}
\whenintegrated{\label{sec:conclusion}} % integrated doc: SST for labels

This paper proposes a simple model linking sovereign default risk, financial repression, and the composition of government debt. Borrowing limits on non-bank creditors and costs of bank absorption jointly determine how debt is allocated across creditor types. When total debt is sufficiently high, composition becomes constrained and shifts mechanically toward the banking sector.
